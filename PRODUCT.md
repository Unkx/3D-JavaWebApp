# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Primary: Requesters** — product designers, engineers, hobbyists, small businesses in Poland who have a digital model (STL) and need it physically produced. They're often on a deadline. They want confidence in the printer's reliability and a quick path to a competitive offer. They are not looking for gig-economy chaos; they expect a platform that matches the precision of what they're ordering.

**Secondary: Printer owners** — hobbyist makers, small print farms, freelance fabricators. They browse open listings and submit offers. They value visibility into job specs (material, size, budget) and a straightforward bidding flow.

## Product Purpose

Druk3D is a Polish marketplace for 3D printing commissions. A requester posts a listing (description, material, budget, optional STL files with live 3D preview), printer owners submit competitive offers, and the requester selects the best fit. The platform removes the friction of finding a trusted local printer — no cold-calling makerspaces, no guessing prices.

Success: a requester can go from "I have an STL" to "I have a confirmed offer" in under five minutes.

## Brand Personality

Bold · Modern · Maker

Voice: Direct and confident without being cold. Speaks to makers and technically-minded people without gatekeeping. Celebrates the craft — 3D printing is still a little magical, and the brand should feel that.

Emotional goal: The requester should feel capable and in control. The printer owner should feel like a professional, not a gig worker.

## Anti-references

- **Fiverr / freelance platforms**: Avoid gig-economy aesthetics — no star ratings on hero, no "starting at $5", no amateur contractor feel. This should feel like a dedicated platform, not a general-purpose marketplace.
- **Generic SaaS (Notion / Linear style)**: Avoid clean-white, blue-sidebar, icon-in-circle-above-heading layouts. The platform is for makers, not knowledge workers. "Productivity tool" aesthetics undersell the craft.

## Design Principles

1. **Show the process** — 3D printing is layered and iterative; the visual language should evoke that precision. Diagrams, cross-sections, and technical visual metaphors belong here; decorative flourishes don't.
2. **Confidence over polish** — Bold typography and committed color choices over safe, watered-down design. Maker culture respects directness.
3. **Speed to value** — Every screen reduces friction. No hero bloat; the most important action is always obvious and reachable.
4. **Trust through specificity** — Copy is concrete, not vague. Sizes, materials, timelines, prices — shown prominently. Trust is built by showing the details, not hiding them behind sales language.
5. **Dark mode as peer** — The platform has printer owners working in workshops at odd hours. Dark mode is a first-class experience, not a cosmetic afterthought.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum (per project CLAUDE.md). Focus management on all modal/dialog interactions. Reduced motion alternative required for every animation. Sufficient contrast for both light and dark themes.
